# Mau Marketing discovery

Status: discovery draft v2
Date: 2026-04-07

## Working idea

**Mau Marketing** would be a NÍTIDA Digital brand focused on marketing services delivered in an AI-native, no-BS way.

Core framing:
- NÍTIDA already sells websites, landing pages, and digital execution.
- Mau Marketing would package web development as a **marketing tool**, not as an isolated build artifact.
- Mau Marketing would also offer selected marketing services beyond web work.
- Positioning should avoid fake guru strategy language and avoid pretending AI can magically do everything.
- Human supervision remains explicit.

## Why this could work

Nuno has:
- 10+ years working with digital agencies from the development/delivery side
- strong understanding of execution quality, process bottlenecks, website UX friction, and what users like/dislike
- the ability to build and automate fast, which can become a competitive operational advantage

This is a stronger foundation than generic “AI marketing agency” positioning.

## Refined positioning hypothesis

Mau Marketing should be positioned as:
- a **marketing-facing NÍTIDA brand**
- execution-heavy rather than theory-heavy
- useful for businesses who are frustrated with weak marketing service, weak websites, weak local visibility, or poor operational follow-up
- playful and irreverent in tone, but serious in delivery

The likely best first clients are not the tiniest “just need a cheap site” clients.
They are more likely to be:
- small local businesses
- businesses willing to pay for marketing help beyond a basic website
- businesses that never had real marketing support
- businesses that are actively unhappy with current providers

## Recommended first-service focus

Based on Nuno’s replies, the strongest initial focus should be:

1. **Landing pages and conversion websites**
2. **Google Business / local visibility / SEO + GTmetrix audits**
3. **EspoCRM setup, customization, and follow-up workflows**

Optional adjacent offer:
- AI integration work where it creates a real operational gain, similar to the more useful AI work already done at NÍTIDA

This is a stronger starting mix than generic “content marketing” because it is:
- easier to explain
- easier to scope
- more operationally credible
- more visibly linked to real business pain

## Services to be careful with initially

Avoid leading with these until there is confidence/process:
- pure brand strategy
- high-level positioning consulting sold as expertise theatre
- performance ads/media buying unless the operational model is truly solid
- SEO sold with broad promises instead of specific audits/fixes
- full social media management with daily content burden

These can expand later, but should not be part of the first promise unless delivery is genuinely strong.

## Packaging ideas

### Productized starting offers

#### A) Landing Page Launch Pack
For businesses with a specific service, campaign, or offer to push.
Includes:
- 1 landing page
- copy structure draft
- form + CRM capture
- thank-you flow
- analytics basics
- one simple follow-up path

Best for:
- local service businesses
- campaigns
- businesses with traffic but poor conversion

#### B) Local Visibility Fix
For small local businesses who are invisible, inconsistent, or badly presented online.
Includes:
- Google Business Profile review/fix
- local SEO quick audit
- GTmetrix/performance audit
- core page/location page recommendations
- implementation of agreed practical fixes

Best for:
- clinics
- shops
- service businesses
- local operators with outdated or weak web presence

#### C) CRM Follow-up Fix
For businesses leaking leads after first contact.
Includes:
- map current lead flow
- EspoCRM setup or customization
- simple pipeline design
- 1–3 automations
- draft follow-up sequence
- optional hosted/managed CRM setup

Best for:
- businesses handling inquiries manually
- businesses with WhatsApp/email chaos
- businesses who know they lose leads after contact

#### D) AI Workflow Add-on
For clients who already have basic systems and need practical automation.
Includes examples like:
- AI-assisted reply drafting
- lead classification support
- content transformation workflows
- internal admin/ops automation

Important:
- do not sell this as a magic feature bundle
- only add it where there is a specific use case and real value

### Suggested retained offers

Retainers should be based on recurring operational value with relatively low weekly hour load and good repeatability.

Best candidates:
- monthly landing page / campaign support
- Google Business and local visibility maintenance
- CRM admin + workflow maintenance
- content refresh / service-page generation batches
- reporting + small monthly optimization rounds
- AI workflow monitoring and iteration

Retainers should avoid becoming vague “we do marketing stuff” containers.
They should be framed as small, useful, recurring systems work.

## Packaging principles

- sell outcomes and bottlenecks, not channels for their own sake
- keep offers small enough to scope and deliver cleanly
- avoid “we do all marketing” vagueness
- make each offer easy to explain in one sentence
- build from audits/sprints into retainers
- make entry offers easy to say yes to
- keep recurring work tightly defined

## What AI can actually do in the business

AI should be treated as an internal production system, QA layer, research helper, and first-draft machine — not as a fake autonomous genius.

### AI roles with high practical value

#### Research and synthesis
- competitor snapshot summaries
- customer review/theme extraction
- FAQ mining
- SERP/topic clustering
- summarizing client materials and past campaign data
- extracting issues and opportunities from weak local business web presence

#### Drafting and variation
- landing page first drafts
- alternate headlines/CTAs
- email sequence drafts
- proposal drafts
- audit summaries
- local SEO/page improvement notes

#### Content transformation
- turn interview notes into site copy
- turn a service page into email/social snippets
- convert long text into FAQ/schema-friendly chunks
- localize/rephrase content for different tones or audiences

#### Workflow acceleration
- internal checklists
- project brief cleanup
- task decomposition
- QA prompts
- report generation
- meeting notes and action extraction
- repeatable landing-page structure generation

#### Technical/ops execution support
- schema markup generation
- meta/title suggestions
- CSV cleanup
- tagging/event-plan suggestions
- automation logic drafting
- support for CRM and no-code/low-code flows
- support for practical AI integrations into client systems

## Where AI should NOT be the promise

Do not promise that AI will:
- replace strategic judgment
- know the client’s business better than they do
- autonomously run campaigns without oversight
- produce perfect copy without editing
- guarantee results

Better framing:
- AI helps Mau Marketing move faster, test more, document better, and deliver more affordably.
- Human supervision keeps quality, context, and responsibility in place.
- Nuno reviews all client work, even if just as a final decision/review layer.

### 5) Pricing logic sketch

Avoid price theatre or over-complex packaging.
Use a simple structure:

- **entry offer**: low-ticket audit/fix package
- **core sprint offers**: fixed-scope packages with clear deliverables
- **retainers**: small monthly recurring packages tied to ongoing support/optimization
- **custom work**: for unusual CRM/AI/system integrations

Pricing should reflect:
- business pain solved
- implementation effort
- customization level
- urgency
- ongoing maintenance burden

Strong rule:
- do not underprice just because AI speeds up some work
- price for value, judgment, speed, and operational leverage
